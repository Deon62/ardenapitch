01

The gap in the market

The problem we’re solving

Peer-to-peer car rental is stuck between informal coordination and one-size-fits-all apps—both sides lose time, trust, and revenue.

Hosts

  • Juggling inquiries across calls, messages, and spreadsheets instead of one clear pipeline.
  • Uncertainty around serious renters, handovers, and disputes when something goes wrong.
  • Payments, pricing, and availability that are hard to manage without dedicated tooling.

Renters

  • Opaque pricing and slow responses make it difficult to book with confidence.
  • Limited trustworthy supply and weak clarity on vehicle condition and pickup.
  • Fragmented experiences—discovery, verification, and payment feel like separate chores.
02

Our approach

The Ardena solution

A single ecosystem with two dedicated apps: hosts run listings, calendars, and payouts in one place; renters discover, verify, and book without the usual friction.

Dual apps, one standard

Interfaces and workflows match how each side works—no bloated menus or compromises from a single generic app.

Trust by design

Clear handovers, vehicle expectations, and dispute-ready documentation so both parties know what “good” looks like.

Money & availability

Pricing, availability, and settlements stay in sync—fewer no-shows, fewer awkward payment threads.

05

Timing

Why now

The infrastructure and habits that make peer-to-peer car rental viable are aligning—waiting means ceding the category to informal workarounds or imports that ignore local reality.

Mobile-first economy

Digital payments, identity checks, and notifications are everyday behavior—ready to underpin verified bookings and predictable handovers.

Supply meets demand

Private vehicles sit underused while urban mobility demand rises; structured rental unlocks value for owners without forcing another car purchase.

Expectations have shifted

People benchmark every service against the best apps they use. Rentals that still run on ad-hoc messages and unclear pricing lose to “good enough” alternatives.
